About Eid

Eid al-Fitr is the first of two major Islamic festivals, marking the end of 
     Ramadan, the holy month of fasting when Muslims abstain from food and drink from 
    dawn until dusk.The name "Eid al-Fitr" translates to "Festival of Breaking the
    Fast" in Arabic, signifying the conclusion of the month-long fast. It is 
    celebrated on the first day of Shawwal, the tenth month of the Islamic lunar
    calendar, and typically lasts for one to three days depending on the country.
    The exact date is determined by the sighting of the new crescent moon after 
    the 29th day of Ramadan, and if the moon is not visible, the celebration is
